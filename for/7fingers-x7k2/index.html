What this is

Most of how this industry works isn't taught anywhere. Especially if you didn't come up through the circus school path. Pay rates, how casting decides, what to ask before signing, how to have a sustainable career. Performers learn it the slow way: through bad contracts, late-night conversations, the friend who was three years ahead.

That's what The Circus Stories is for. We run the What Does Circus Pay survey, interview people across the industry, and are building a career course. We want to create useful resources for performers, and beyond that share the kinds of stories most people never hear about this work. By Alicia and Deelia of Duo Illumi, performers ourselves.

Approval & publishing

Every piece of content that includes Louis or references The 7 Fingers is sent to the company for written approval before publication. Nothing goes live without explicit sign-off, per piece. Approval can be granted, revised, or revoked at any stage before publication.

Once we've edited the recording, we send 7 Fingers everything in one batch: the long-form video, every short clip, every carousel, every pull-quote graphic, the website article. The team reviews per piece. Anything not approved doesn't get used.

If something is approved and circumstances change, the company can revoke approval before publication and we won't publish it. The interview rushes are kept and shared with the company on request.

A note on reusing content. Once a clip or quote has been approved and published, we'd like to repost it later in different formats. For example, an approved YouTube clip turned into a Reel, or a quote pulled into the newsletter or course. The content stays the same, just shows up in a different place. If we'd ever want to use something in a way that changes the meaning or framing, we'd come back and ask.

What we won't do
  • Publish anything from this interview before written approval from The 7 Fingers
  • Use Louis's words, image or The 7 Fingers' name in any third-party advertising, sponsorship, or commercial promotion
  • Frame the interview as exclusive 7 Fingers casting reveals or any framing that could damage the company's reputation
  • Release outtakes, raw footage, or any material that wasn't explicitly approved
  • Use AI or editing to alter what was said beyond standard length and clarity edits, which are also subject to approval
  • Sell access to the interview itself or lock it behind a paywall

Note on the course. The career course will be paid, but every quote and clip used in it will also be available for free on YouTube, Instagram, and the website. Not as lead magnets or behind email signups — just published as freely available content.
